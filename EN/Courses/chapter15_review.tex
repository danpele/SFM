\section{Course Summary}

\begin{frame}{Course Summary}
    \begin{itemize}
        \item Overview of all topics covered in Chapters 1--14
        \item Efficient Market Hypothesis and random walk models
        \item Variance ratio tests and stylized facts of financial returns
        \item Volatility modeling: ARCH, GARCH, and asymmetric extensions
        \item Risk measures: VaR and Expected Shortfall
        \item Credit scoring and machine learning in finance
        \item Cryptocurrency markets and stablecoins
        \item Systemic risk measurement and network analysis
        \item Fractal Market Hypothesis: Hurst exponent and long memory
    \end{itemize}
\end{frame}

\section{Key Concepts}

\begin{frame}{Key Concepts}
    \begin{itemize}
        \item Market efficiency and its three forms
        \item Random walk types (RW1, RW2, RW3) and their tests
        \item Stylized facts: heavy tails, volatility clustering, leverage effect
        \item GARCH family: specification, estimation, model selection
        \item VaR estimation methods and backtesting
        \item Logistic regression and ROC/AUC for scoring models
        \item LASSO, Random Forest, and model validation
        \item CoVaR, FRM, and systemic risk networks
        \item Fractal Market Hypothesis, R/S analysis, and DFA
    \end{itemize}
\end{frame}
