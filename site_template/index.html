Question 1: Returns

What is the advantage of using log returns over simple returns?

  • A) They are always positive
  • B) They are time-additive and approximately normally distributed
  • C) They are easier to calculate
  • D) They are always larger than simple returns
Correct! Log returns are time-additive (multi-period returns = sum of single-period log returns) and tend to be closer to normally distributed.
Incorrect. The correct answer is B. Log returns have the property of time-additivity and approximate normality.
